I am a researcher and PhD candidate in the Human-Centered Computing (HCC) program at Georgia Tech. I am a member of the Information Interfaces Research Group , which is part of the broader Georgia Tech Visualization Lab . My PhD advisor is Dr. John Stasko . Before pursuing my PhD, I worked as a sports journalist and analyst and earned a Master's degree in Electrical Engineering.

Research Overview

My research spans information visualization, data science, human-computer interaction, and data journalism, with a focus on three key dimensions: designing analytical interfaces that leverage interactive visualizations to support data exploration and extract actionable insights across different domains; developing authoring software powered by Generative AI to streamline the creation of engaging and accurate data-driven content (e.g., visual data stories); and countering data-driven misinformation by understanding how data-driven communication can mislead people through flawed narratives or visuals, and developing strategies, designs, and tools to address these challenges.
